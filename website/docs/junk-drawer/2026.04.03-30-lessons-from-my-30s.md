My wife mentioned that I should write a book about the things that I learned as I went 
through my 30s. Some of these lessons, I feel that had I learned them sooner, like in my 
teens or 20s, that I would have not gotten the same value or level of education that 
I got from learning these things in my 30s. 

These lessons are not in any particular order, but I do consider all of the lessons as being 
valuable. Each is valuable in their own regard. Some lessons go in conjunction with 
other lessons. 

As I enter into my 40s, below are the lessons from my years past that I believe 
others could learn from.  Whether you have already passed your 30s or have yet to venture 
into them, we can all learn from each other.
