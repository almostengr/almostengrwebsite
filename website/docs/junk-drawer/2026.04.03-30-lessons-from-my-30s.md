My wife mentioned that I should write a book about the things that I learned as I went 
through my 30s. Some of these lessons, I feel that had I learned them sooner, like in my 
teens or 20s, that I would have not gotten the same value or level of education that 
I got from learning these things in my 30s. 

These lessons are not in any particular order, but I do consider all of the lessons as being 
valuable. Each is valuable in their own regard. Some lessons go in conjunction with 
other lessons. 

As I enter into my 40s, below are the lessons from my years past that I believe 
others could learn from.  Whether you have already passed your 30s or have yet to venture 
into them, we can all learn from each other.

* Baby Sign Language
* Social Anxiety Disorder 
* Understanding Your Attachment Style
* Eat Less Sugar
* MBTI (Myers-Briggs Type Indicator)
* All Blood Isn't Good Blood
* Ennegram
* 5 Love Languages
* Discernment / Hearing Holy Spirit
* Mental Health and Therapist
* Depression Signs and Indicators
* Ask For Clarification
* Never Stop Learning
* Know Your Family Medical History
* Men Need Space to Think
* Credit and Lexis Nexis Report
* Like a Plant, You Need Sunlight
* Kids Need A Schedule
* Practice Public Speaking, Toastmasters
* Working with a Different Degree
* Don't Leave God, Leave That Church
* Receive Correction
* Braces in Adulthood
* Leave That Job
* Write Your Personal History
* Parent Is A Child's First Teacher
* Learn After You Graduate
* No Tech Required to Work In Tech
